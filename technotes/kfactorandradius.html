Example

Let us take the example, where thickness is 1.5mm, desired radius (RD) is 2.1mm and interior angle of 90°.

From the table, V width range is 7-22mm and desired V width based on RD(2.1mm) is 12mm.

Note: If die is suggested in geo file, above step is skipped.

Now, based on thickness, V width and interior angle, bend radius and deduction are computed.

Bend radius computation

For Trumpf machines, TBS is used to compute bend radius (RA).

From given V width, actual radius at 90° (RA) is computed by multiplying constant 0.16 with V width.

So, for the given Example,

RA = 0.16 * 12 = 1.92mm

For angles other than 90°, radius is computed as below.

Angle less than 90°

For a given angle 45° radius factor is computed using interpolation.

radius factor = interpolated radius (RI) / calculated radius at 90° (RC90)

RI = interpolation between the punch tip radius and the effective radius at 90° (RC90)

= Punch tip radius * (1 - interpolation lie) + calculated radius at 90° * interpolation lie

Punch tip radius is 1mm for the given thickness 1.5mm, calculated using below table.

For angle 45°, the interpolation lie between 30° and 90° is

interpolation lie = (45 - 30) / (90 - 30) = 0.25

RC90 = 1.92,

so applying these,

RI = 1 * (1 - 0.25) + 1.92 * 0.25 = 1.23

∴ radius factor = 1.23 / 1.92 = 0.640625

∴ actual radius at 45° (RA) = RA90 * radius factor = 1.92 * 0.640625 = 1.23

In the above Example, if the interior angle is 135°, inner bend radius (RA) is calculated by multiplying radius factor 2.32 with actual radius at 90° (RA90). Thus,

RA = radius factor * RA90 = 2.32 * 1.92 = 4.45mm

K factor computation

For Trumpf machines, TBS is used to compute bend deduction and from that K factor is calculated.

Based on K factor table in Material settings (Database → Material) K factor is computed and from that bend deduction is computed.

See below for sample table:

Ratio (Inner radius / Thickness)

K-Factor

1

0.38

2

0.43

3

0.5

In the Example, inner radius for 90° is 1.92mm and thickness is 1.5mm. So the ratio is 1.28.

We have to now compute K factor for 1.28 by interpolation.

Let the required intervals of K factor be Ka = 0.38 and Kb = 0.43 and the intervals of ratio be Ratioa = 1 and Ratiob = 2.

K factor = Ka * (1 - lie of the ratio) + Kb * lie of the ratio

lie of the ratio between Ratioa and Ratiob = (1.28 - Ratioa) / (Ratiob - Ratioa) = 1.28 - 1 / 2 - 1 = 0.28

∴ K factor = 0.38 * (1 - 0.28) + 0.43 * 0.28 = 0.394
