Checklist for Documenters

  • To develop high-quality documentation, we must adhere to certain standards. As a result, we use a checklist to guarantee that all document quality parameters are met.

  • Documenters must utilize this checklist after editing content and do a self-check. In some circumstances (when creating a lengthy document), a peer review can also help to avoid the most trivial errors.

  • A pull request should not be sent for review without self-check or peer review.

The checklist below will cover basic quality characteristics:

Accuracy

  • Is all the information documented correct and error-free?

  • Have you meticulously checked the content for any inaccuracies, typographical errors, or misleading statements?

  • Have you cross-checked facts, figures, and data against reliable sources?

  • Is all the information presented based on verified and trustworthy references?

  • Are software UI elements (button names, menu items, labels) documented correctly?

Clarity and Readability

  • Is the language clear, concise, and free of jargon?

  • Is active voice used throughout the document?

  • Is the grammar, spelling, and punctuation correct?

  • Are sentences well-structured and easy to understand?

  • Are appropriate headings and subheadings used for better readability?

  • Used bullet points or numbered lists wherever required to enhance readability?

  • Are software features explained with examples?

Consistency

  • Is approved terminology used in the document? (Refer to inTerm)

  • Are the same terms and definitions used consistently to avoid confusion?

  • Does the document adhere to a uniform writing style and tone that aligns with its purpose and audience?

  • Are abbreviations and acronyms defined the first time they appear?

  • Are all figures and tables labeled consistently?

  • Are software UI elements (button names, menu items, labels) named consistently?

Usability

  • Are diagrams, charts, and images relevant and well-integrated?

  • Are cross references working correctly?

  • Is cross reference easy to navigate between topics or pages?

  • Are the navigation buttons and page-wise TOC working properly?

Localization

  • Are the sentences, words and verbs easy to translate?

  • Translation IDs (Anchor points) for heading and subheadings created wherever required?
